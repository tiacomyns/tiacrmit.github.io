Project Idea

A great project idea that really demonstrates the sort of innovation I would like to see in the tech industry, is the cost and accessibility of prosthetics for those who cant really afford the standard prosthetic price tag. I believe that an Arduino, a #D printed hand and a few other parts would be a cost effective way of bringing prosthetics to people who need them.

The thing about prosthetics is that they are expensive, costing hundreds to thousands of dollars to buy excluding any form of maintenance costs that may need to be dealt with after a bit of wear and tear. They are also so inaccessible for people in third world and war torn countries, there are over 1.5 million people in Syria living with permanent war related damage, over 86,000 of those people have lost limbs, and I bet most of them cant afford to get themselves an expensive prosthetic. The Arduino run and 3d printed prosthetic is a great solution as it is far cheaper to make than a standard prosthetic and hence would become more accessible and affordable for people like us here in Australia to donate prosthetics to these people who need them most.
